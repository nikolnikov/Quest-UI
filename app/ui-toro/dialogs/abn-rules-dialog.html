ABN Rules
Medicare will only pay for services that it determines to be reasonable and necessary under section 1862 (a) (1) of the Medicare Law. If Medicare determines that a particular service, although it would otherwise be covered, is not reasonable and necessary under the Medicare Program standards, Medicare will deny payment for that service.

Explain to the patient that tests ordered by his/her physician and identified on this requisition with the following symbols are likely to be denied for payment for the reason(s) indicated:
  • @ = May not be covered for the reported condition
  • F = Has prescribed frequency rules for coverage
  • & = A test or service performed with research/experimental kit
  • B = Has both diagnosis and frequency-related coverage limitations
Details regarding National frequency limitations are included in the National Coverage Determinations available on the CMS Website at http://www.cms.hhs.gov/coverage/labindexlist.asp.

For the covered screening services, the National Medicare Frequency guidelines are as follows:
  • Occult Blood, PSA and High Risk Pap Smear - once every 12 months
  • Non-High Risk Pap Smear - once every 24 months
After the ABN has printed, ensure the patient has read and understands the form. Notify the patient that, by signing the ABN, he/she is confirming their agreement to assume financial responsibility for the payment of these tests if they are denied by Medicare.
